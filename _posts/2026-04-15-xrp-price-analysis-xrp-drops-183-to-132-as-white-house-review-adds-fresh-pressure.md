XRP’s biggest problem isn’t the chart—it’s the policy overhang. The token slid **1.83% to $1.32**, and with the **SEC safe harbor proposal now under White House review**, traders are treating every bounce as fragile until Washington gives the market something firmer to price in.

That matters because XRP is still a major asset by size: its market cap sits near **$80.8 billion**, even after a weak stretch that includes a **-1.64% move over 7 days** and **-3.05% over 30 days**. But scale alone hasn’t translated into conviction. Instead, XRP keeps trading in a tight, cautious band around the **$1.33 area**, with short-term momentum still soft and the market unwilling to call a trend change.

The regulatory backdrop is the real driver. The latest round of headlines suggests U.S. crypto policy is still being actively shaped, not settled. That keeps XRP’s legal narrative front and center, especially because the asset’s value proposition has always been tied closely to how regulators classify and treat it. Until that uncertainty fades, price action is likely to remain reactive rather than decisive.
