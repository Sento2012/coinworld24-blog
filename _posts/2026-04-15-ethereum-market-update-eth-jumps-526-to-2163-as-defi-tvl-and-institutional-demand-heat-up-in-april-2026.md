Ethereum’s latest push looks less like a quick squeeze and more like a broad-based confidence trade. ETH is holding near **$2,163.98**, up **5.26% in 24 hours**, while its **7-day gain of 4.41%** and **30-day rise of 9.22%** suggest the move is being sustained by more than noise.

What stands out most is the overlap between price strength and on-chain fundamentals. DeFi liquidity is expanding again: **Aave V3 TVL increased 4.05% to $24.46 billion**, **Lido rose 6.20% to $20.16 billion**, and **SSV Network climbed 5.43% to $15.83 billion**. That matters because it shows capital is actively flowing into lending, staking, and validator infrastructure rather than sitting idle.

The institutional backdrop is also turning more supportive. The **Ethereum Foundation has staked 69,500 ETH**, nearly reaching its **70,000 ETH** target, reinforcing the idea that staking yield and network security remain a core part of Ethereum’s long-term strategy. At the same time, fresh access points could widen demand further in **2026**, with Charles Schwab reportedly preparing direct spot bitcoin and ether trading. Large treasury-style accumulation is adding another layer, too: Bitmine reportedly bought **71,252 ETH** in its biggest weekly purchase since December, while its ether holdings have reached **4.8 million ETH**.

The result is a market structure that looks healthier than a simple momentum burst. ETH is being supported by higher TVL, stronger staking participation, and a growing institutional bridge into the asset. That combination could help explain why bulls are watching the **$2,200** area so closely.
