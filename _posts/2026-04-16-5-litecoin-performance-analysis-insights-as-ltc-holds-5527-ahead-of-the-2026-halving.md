Litecoin’s near-term chart is still soft, but the market structure is starting to look more constructive than the 30-day decline suggests. LTC is holding around **$55.27** after falling **5.96% over the past month**, yet it has already posted a modest rebound of **+2.16% in 24 hours** and **+2.67% over 7 days**. That combination points to buyers defending the lower end of the range even as broader altcoin sentiment remains uneven.

The more important variable is supply. Litecoin has **77,061,996 coins** circulating out of a fixed **84,000,000 maximum**, which leaves relatively little issuance left compared with earlier cycles. With the next halving expected in **2026**, the long-term thesis is increasingly about reduced supply growth rather than short-term momentum. That kind of setup tends to matter more for patient capital than for traders looking for immediate breakout confirmation.

Network fundamentals also support the base case. Rising hash rate trends typically indicate stronger miner participation and improved chain security ahead of supply shocks, which can reinforce confidence when price action is otherwise flat. Positioning matters too. LTC still ranks **#25 by market cap**, with about **$4.26 billion** in market value and roughly **$367.6 million** in daily volume, which keeps it liquid enough to benefit if capital rotates into established large-cap altcoins. In that sense, Litecoin is less of a momentum trade and more of a scarcity-and-durability trade heading into the 2026 halving window.
